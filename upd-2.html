Universal Principles of Design

Sound design is not only within the reach of a small set of uniquely talented individuals, but can be achieved by virtually all designers. The use of well-established design principles increases the probability that a design will be successful. Use Universal Principles of Design as a resource to increase your cross-disciplinary knowledge and understanding of design, promote brainstorming and idea generation for design problems, and refresh your memory of design principles that are infrequently applied. Finally, use it as a means of checking the quality of your design process and product.

These principles address the question “How can I help people learn from a design?“ Click each principle for a definition and page reference.

  • Exposure Effect
    • Repeated exposure to stimuli for which people have neutral feelings will increase the likeability of the stimuli. (pp. 86-87)
  • Forgiveness
    • Designs should help people avoid errors and minimize the negative consequences of errors when they do occur. (pp. 104-105)
  • Garbage In–Garbage Out
    • The quality of system output is dependent on the quality of system input. (pp. 112-113)
  • Hierarchy
    • Hierarchical organization is the simplest structure for visualizing and understanding complexity. (pp. 122-123)
  • Immersion
    • A state of mental focus so intense that awareness of the “real” world is lost, generally resulting in a feeling of joy and satisfaction. (pp. 134-135)
  • Interference Effects
    • A phenomenon in which mental processing is made slower and less accurate by competing mental processes. (pp. 138-139)
  • Inverted Pyramid
    • A method of information presentation in which information is presented in descending order of importance. (pp. 140-141)
  • Layering
    • The process of organizing information into related groupings in order to manage complexity and reinforce relationships in the information. (pp.146-147)
